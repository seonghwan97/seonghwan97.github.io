My name is Seonghwan Lim, and I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in Computer Science at the Illinois Institute of Technology. Research Interests

My research interests lie in computer vision, multimodal sensing, and intelligent interactive systems. I am particularly interested in building systems that extract meaningful signals from visual and sensor data and use them for prediction, interpretation, and real-time adaptation in complex environments.

  • Vision-based modeling for fine-grained and real-world recognition problems, including long-tailed and challenging visual datasets.
  • Human-centered perception systems that estimate pose, movement, energy expenditure, and cognitive or behavioral state from multi-camera or monocular inputs.
  • Real-time embodied and spatially aware systems that connect perception, visualization, and control.
  • Practical machine learning pipelines that combine robust data processing, feature extraction, predictive modeling, and deployment-oriented system design.

More broadly, I aim to develop intelligent systems that bridge perception and decision-making, especially in settings where human behavior, physical environments, and real-time feedback need to be jointly understood.
